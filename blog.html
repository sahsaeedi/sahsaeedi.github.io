Aggregating Visual Embeddings in Large Language Models

  • Agneet Chatterjee

    • Arizona State University

Generative models have become ubiquitous, and for good reason. The multitudinous variety of GPTâ€™s and Diffusion models have truly enabled a step towards artificial general intelligence, with their impressive generalization abilities. However, they are currently restricted to only generate uni-modal outputs. Recent models from DeepMind and OpenAI have gone a step forward and allowed for ingestion of multimodal inputs, however the aforementioned restriction still applies to them. Recently, an effort has been made, to produce multi-modal outputs, however they do not generate novel images, rather retrieve them from a fixed database.

We explore a simple hypothesis. Can we leverage only the generalization abilities of large language models and empower them to generate visual outputs together with text, in an auto-regressive fashion? To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to explore such a task, in an end-to-end fashion, generating images and texts, in an interleaved fashion. Such a model would enable hierarchical and multimodal coherency, across all pairs of generated texts and images.
